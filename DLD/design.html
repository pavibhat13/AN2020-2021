2020 and the New Normal
Cargill had a phenomenal start of the fiscal year 20-21, as we were scaling the heights, we were faced with an unprecedented disruption that we and the world were less prepared for—the Covid-19 pandemic. Within a year of its deadly presence, it had consumed the lives and livelihoods of millions of people across the world. Economies stumped and organizations were grappling to sustain amidst the chaos that ensued, causing a radical shift in how they operate. 
Working remotely became the ‘new normal’ and Cargill quickly mobilized efforts to provide a safe working environment for its 165,000 strong workforce... The activities that followed were akin to wartime effort—sending thousands of laptops to our scattered personnel, arranging internet connectivity, access to medical aid, counseling, and even making room for furniture to ensure a comfortable working environment in the safety of their homes. The safety of our employees was non-negotiable.​
Our team soon bounced back to doing what we do best—deliver on our commitments. We had to devise new means of connecting with our customers and stakeholders to understand their innate needs—this was the core of customized content development. The office soon became a virtual space; meetings, coffee breaks, and friendly banter were all online. We dedicated time to check in, the daily scrum calls became a forum for the team to highlight challenges they were facing and the support they required. With a geographically scattered team, it was imperative that we had regular connects, understood pain points, supported, and motivated each other, course corrected and worked in unison. Being ‘agile’ helped us in planning and forecasting and therefore, we were able to deliver on our commitments without a hitch. We supported each other, albeit remotely, leveraging technology to connect and extend support. Virtual meetings and coffee chats with the larger team and Cargill leadership erased boundaries and brought us together amidst this massive disruption to understand the larger picture and the direction taken by the organization.

Embracing HTML, The New Normal
Responsive designs are essential aspects of development in this digital era. In our attempt to provide an optimum learner experience (LX) across devices, we have adopted fully responsive HTML development.

Leveraging Native HTML in building content offers learners a seamless learning experience with greater engagement, which is both visually appealing and easier to navigate. We use it to create fully responsive learning content that is compatible across devices and operating systems. Not just that, by leveraging analytical data, we optimize the design and build, elevating the learning experience. The translate attribute in HTML helps learners better understand the content and helps save significant costs incurred for translation.

Driving the Quality of Learning through Innovation
“Sometimes when you innovate, you make mistakes. It is best to admit them quickly and get on with improving your other innovations.” – Steve Jobs

In order to stay relevant in this fast-changing work environment and to differentiate ourselves from the competition, it is important for us to think creatively, to discuss, and to share ideas with one another now more than ever. To keep up with the tectonic shift in learners’ needs and learning modalities, our team uses the design thinking framework to explore and test different ideas. Projects that formed part of our innovation initiative include:
ChatBot
Anticipating the needs of stakeholders and attempting to fulfill them are the hallmarks of a growth-oriented team. As a formative step in that direction, we developed “VEDA”—Virtual E-Learning Digital Assistant. VEDA was designed integrating the nuances of human interaction and AI, offering a novel way to reach out to our team. VEDA is equipped to answer FAQs and questions pertaining to tools, services, and products of the Digital Design team. VEDA is accessible on Microsoft Teams and anyone with a Teams account can explore VEDA.
Project Status: In Progress
AutoTranslate
Our long-term vision to optimize the holy trinity of cost, quality, and time in the globalization of our learning assets led us to a pivotal breakthrough—the integration of Google Translate API to HTML courseware. This allows us to translate any (HTML) asset across browsers, devices and operating systems instantly, and at no cost.
Project Status: Completed
Digital Learning Design: Portfolio
The Digital Design team's portfolio site encompasses the creative competencies of the Digital Design team, together with information on our current and upcoming projects, technology trends adopted by us and our success stories.
Project Status: In Progress
Style Guide for American English | A handbook on American spelling and punctuation
The American English Style Guide is a reference document that enables content writers and instructional designers to identify the subtle differences in the spelling convention and punctuation that exist between British and American writing styles
Project Status: In Progress
Live Project tracker
‘What gets measured, gets improved’—this becomes critical as the team’s roles and responsibilities are enhanced in both scope and scale. We have developed a live project tracker to create a real-time snapshot of the constantly evolving data. This intends to serve as the single source of truth for the leadership, stakeholders, and our team members in the long run. Currently it is 90% automated and well-integrated with multiple data sources. The focus here is to enable quick and easy access to information to the stakeholders.
Project Status: In Progress

Artificial Intelligence
❮  Prev
9/10
Next  ❯
It is predicted that through 2021, AI could become the primary trend and will grow by more than 45%.  AI is probably the single most important technological advancement of the coming century. It has impacted every area of our civilization and learning is no exception. There’s a lot of impressive work being done on the AI front within Cargill. Staying abreast of this technology trend, DLD has also leveraged AI in some of its projects... 

GPT: We’ve created a working prototype of the GPT2 which can generate text based on prompts. GPT is an autoregressive natural language processing model relying on transformer-based deep learning neural network architecture. You can find our innovation canvas submission here, read more about the underlying tech and find some ideas to explore on your own.
This experiment in DLD is still in its nascent stages and we are working towards solving challenges w.r.t test data and compute power to proceed further. We would like to thank Evert, Shawn, Santhosh and Pavithra, along with Digital Labs and CDP teams for all the guidance, support and work thus far.

VEDA FAQ Bot: This is still in the beta phase, and you can try it out in action. We’ve used a low/no code platform, Microsoft Power Virtual Agent, which uses AI-based algorithms to continually optimize the responses.
This bot also comes with built-in analytics capabilities to assess the interactions with the users and is intended to reduce manual overhead for early stage and exploratory conversations between the L&D team and stakeholders. It also informs and educates those outside our team to get quick, consistent and useful information at the click of a button(mouse).
